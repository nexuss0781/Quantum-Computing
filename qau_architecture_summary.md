# Quantum Absolute Unit (QAU) Architecture Summary

This document summarizes the proposed architecture for the Quantum Absolute Unit (QAU) and its foundational components, the Quantum Primordials, as described in the provided specification. The QAU is envisioned as a **Quantum Virtual Substrate (QVS)**, an operating system layer designed to execute these primordials natively on silicon, moving beyond the simulation paradigm of quantum mechanics.

## I. The Three Quantum Primordials

The QAU is built upon three irreducible computational essences, termed **Quantum Primordials**, which cannot be decomposed further without losing quantumness. These primordials are the Amplitude Superposition Cell (ASC), the Relative Phase Weave (RPW), and the Non-Local Correlation Bond (NCB).

### 1. Amplitude Superposition Cell (ASC)

The **Amplitude Superposition Cell (ASC)** serves as the primitive of **coherent multiplicity**. It functions as a computational unit capable of simultaneously holding multiple values, $\{v_1, v_2, ..., v_n\}$, each associated with complex weights, $\{\alpha_1, \alpha_2, ..., \alpha_n\}$, such that the sum of the squared magnitudes of these weights, $\sum |\alpha_i|^2$, equals one. On silicon, the ASC is not merely an array but is implemented as a **lazy tensor block**. This data structure efficiently stores only the non-zero amplitudes and their corresponding indices, representing a sparse form. However, it is designed to perform arithmetic operations as if it were a full $2^n$ vector, thereby embodying the mathematical essence of quantum parallelism within DRAM.

### 2. Relative Phase Weave (RPW)

The **Relative Phase Weave (RPW)** embodies the primitive of **interference**, where the phase $e^{i\theta}$ is considered a fundamental computational element rather than mere metadata. It establishes a continuous rotational relationship between ASCs, which is critical for enabling constructive or destructive amplification of quantum paths. This mechanism is essential for manifesting true quantum behavior, distinguishing it fundamentally from classical probability. In its silicon implementation, the RPW is represented by a floating-point angle $\theta$ linked to each basis state. This angle is manipulated using **geometric algebra** (rotors), a method chosen to circumvent the computational overhead associated with trigonometric functions while rigorously maintaining phase coherence across all operations.

### 3. Non-Local Correlation Bond (NCB)

The **Non-Local Correlation Bond (NCB)** acts as a primitive that enforces **informational constraint**, fundamentally representing entanglement. This bond ensures that joint probability distributions, $P(a,b)$, cannot be factorized into independent probabilities, $P(a)P(b)$, thereby causing two ASCs to behave as a single, unified computational object. On silicon, the NCB is realized as a **shared constraint pointer** that links two ASC blocks. This linkage ensures that any operation performed on one ASC instantaneously updates the joint amplitude tensor of the other, without requiring explicit data copying. This intricate interaction is simulated effectively through reference-counted tensor networks.
